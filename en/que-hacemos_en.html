What We Do

Our projects and action lines for marine conservation

Ecosystem Protection

We actively work on the protection and recovery of vulnerable marine ecosystems such as coral reefs, posidonia meadows, and breeding areas for threatened species.

Our projects include the creation of marine reserves, restoration of degraded habitats, and continuous monitoring of ecosystem health.

Marine Wildlife Rescue and Rehabilitation

In collaboration with specialized centers, we participate in the rescue and rehabilitation of marine species affected by pollution, accidental fishing, or strandings.

We have a rapid response team to handle emergencies related to:

  • Sea turtles
  • Cetaceans
  • Seabirds
  • Endangered species

Scientific Research

We develop scientific studies to expand knowledge about marine ecosystems and the threats they face.

Our main research lines include:

  • Impact of microplastics on marine fauna
  • Effects of climate change on Mediterranean coral reefs
  • Monitoring of threatened species populations
  • Study of water quality in coastal areas
  • Development of innovative techniques for ecosystem restoration
